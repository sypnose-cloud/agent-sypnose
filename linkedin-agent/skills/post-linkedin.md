# Skill: LinkedIn Post

Write a LinkedIn post that gets read. Proven structure, no filler.

## Structure

1. **Hook** (1-2 lines) — provocative, specific, surprising
2. **Story or data** (3-4 lines) — real, personal, concrete
3. **Lesson or insight** (2-3 lines) — what you learned or built
4. **CTA** (1 line) — one question or invitation, never beg for likes

## Weekly rotation

| Day | Topic |
|-----|-------|
| Monday | Real use case — what you built this week |
| Wednesday | Technical insight — something you learned |
| Friday | Opinion — AI in production, what actually works |

## Voice rules

- Direct and compressed — no filler words
- Numbers and specifics beat adjectives
- No "Excited to share", no "Not X just Y"
- No fake curiosity hooks
- Sounds like a builder, not a LinkedIn influencer

## Example prompt

```
Write a LinkedIn post for Wednesday.
Topic: I automated my job search with an AI agent.
Voice: direct, technical, real numbers.
Structure: hook → what I built → what I learned → one question.
```
